These topics are handpicked because ArXiv has **dense, conflicting literature** on all of them — perfect for generating a real debate between the agents.

## 🤖 Artificial Intelligence

| Topic | Why it debates well |
|---|---|
| `Can large language models truly reason or are they just pattern matching?` | Huge split between scaling optimists and cognitive scientists |
| `Does reinforcement learning from human feedback make AI safer?` | RLHF is everywhere but critics say it just masks bad behavior |
| `Can AI replace radiologists?` | Geoffrey Hinton said yes in 2016 — papers have been fighting ever since |
| `Does AI accelerate scientific discovery?` | AlphaFold camp vs. those who say AI finds correlations not causes |
| `Will scaling laws continue to hold for large language models?` | Core tension between labs betting billions and skeptical researchers |

## 🧬 Biology & Medicine

| Topic | Why it debates well |
|---|---|
| `Do psychedelics treat depression better than SSRIs?` | Promising trials vs. serious methodological concerns |
| `Is CRISPR gene editing safe for humans?` | Off-target edits and the He Jiankui fallout fuel both sides |
| `Can intermittent fasting extend human lifespan?` | Animal data is strong, human data is messy |
| `Does the gut microbiome influence mental health?` | Gut-brain axis research is exploding but causation is disputed |
| `Are mRNA vaccines the future of all medicine?` | COVID success vs. questions about long-term immune response |

| `Does dark matter exist or do we need a new theory of gravity?` | MOND vs. ΛCDM — one of the biggest open fights in cosmology |

## 💡 Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific** — `Can AI replace radiologists in breast cancer screening?` debates better than `Is AI good at medicine?`
- **Frame it as a question** — the agents are prompted to argue for and against, so a yes/no framing works best
- **Controversial > consensus** — topics where the science is still unsettled produce the most interesting debates
